A [business line of credit](/business-loans/lines-of-credit) is one of the most flexible financing products available to small business owners. Unlike a term loan that gives you a lump sum, a line of credit lets you draw funds as needed, repay them, and draw again without reapplying. You only pay interest on what you actually use.

For businesses that deal with fluctuating cash flow, seasonal inventory needs, or unpredictable expenses, a line of credit is often the smartest first financing product to establish.

## How a Business Line of Credit Works

Think of a business line of credit like a business credit card with better rates and higher limits. Here is how the mechanics work:

**Draw period.** Once approved, you have access to your credit limit for a set period, typically 12 to 36 months. During this time, you can draw funds whenever you need them.

**Interest on what you use.** If you are approved for $100,000 but only draw $30,000, you pay interest on $30,000. The remaining $70,000 costs you nothing until you use it.

**Revolving access.** As you repay what you borrowed, that amount becomes available to borrow again. This revolving structure makes it ideal for ongoing needs rather than one-time purchases.

**Secured vs. unsecured.** Secured lines require collateral (equipment, real estate, accounts receivable) and generally offer lower rates and higher limits. Unsecured lines do not require collateral but typically come with higher rates and lower limits.

## Business Line of Credit Requirements

Qualification requirements vary by lender, but here is what most lenders evaluate:

**Time in business.** Most lenders require at least 6 to 12 months in operation. Banks and SBA lenders typically want 2+ years. Online lenders are more flexible with newer businesses.

**Annual revenue.** Minimums range from $50,000 to $250,000 depending on the lender. Higher revenue generally means higher credit limits and better rates.

**Credit score.** Minimum scores typically range from 580 (online lenders) to 680+ (banks and SBA). According to <a href="https://www.experian.com/blogs/ask-experian/research/business-loan-statistics/" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Experian's business lending data</a>, borrowers with credit scores above 700 receive significantly better terms.

**Cash flow.** Lenders want to see consistent bank deposits and positive cash flow. Most will review 3 to 6 months of bank statements.

**Documentation.** Basic requirements include bank statements, tax returns, business financial statements, and proof of business ownership. See our full [loan document checklist](/tools/loan-document-checklist) for specifics.

## What It Actually Costs

Business line of credit costs include several components:

**Interest rates:** 7% to 25% APR for most products. Banks and SBA offer the low end. Online lenders fall in the middle to upper range.

**Draw fees:** Some lenders charge 1% to 2% each time you draw funds. This adds to your effective cost.

**Maintenance fees:** Annual or monthly fees ranging from $0 to $175/year. Not all lenders charge these.

**Inactivity fees:** Some lenders charge a fee if you do not use the line for an extended period. Ask about this before signing.

## Business Line of Credit vs. Term Loan

| Factor | Line of Credit | Term Loan |
|---|---|---|
| **Structure** | Revolving, draw as needed | Lump sum upfront |
| **Interest** | Only on amount drawn | On full loan amount |
| **Repayment** | Flexible, revolving | Fixed monthly payments |
| **Best for** | Ongoing and variable needs | One-time investments |
| **Rates** | 7% to 25% | 7% to 30% |
| **Reusability** | Yes, funds replenish | No, one-time disbursement |

**Choose a line of credit when:** Your needs are ongoing, variable, or unpredictable. Cash flow gaps, inventory purchases, seasonal expenses, or having a safety net available.

**Choose a [term loan](/business-loans/term-loans) when:** You have a specific one-time need with a clear cost. Expansion, renovation, major equipment, or debt consolidation.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

### 1. Check Your Financial Position

Before applying anywhere, know where you stand. Check your personal credit score, review your business bank statements for the last 6 months, and calculate your [DSCR](/tools/dscr-calculator). This tells you what you can realistically qualify for and what you can afford to carry.

### 2. Gather Your Documentation

Preparing documents in advance speeds up the process significantly. Most lenders need:

- 3 to 6 months of business bank statements
- Last 2 years of business tax returns
- Profit and loss statement
- Balance sheet
- Business license and EIN
- Personal identification

Our [loan document checklist](/tools/loan-document-checklist) breaks this down by lender type.

### 3. Compare Multiple Lenders

Do not accept the first offer. Compare at least 3 lenders on APR, fees, credit limit, draw period, and repayment terms. Our [loan offer analyzer](/tools/loan-offer-analyzer) helps you compare offers side by side. The <a href="https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/blog/shopping-for-a-small-business-loan-cfpb-small-business-lending-resources/" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)</a> recommends comparing total cost of borrowing, not just the stated rate.

### 4. Submit Your Application

Once you have identified the right lender, submit your application with all documentation. Online lenders can approve in 24 to 48 hours. Banks and SBA lenders may take 2 to 6 weeks.

### 5. Review and Accept Terms

Read every term before signing. Pay specific attention to: the interest rate (fixed or variable), all fees, the draw period length, repayment terms, and whether there are prepayment penalties. If anything is unclear, ask before you sign.

## How to Use a Line of Credit Strategically

A business line of credit is a tool. Like any tool, how you use it determines the outcome.

**Bridge cash flow gaps.** Cover payroll or expenses during slow periods when you know receivables are coming. This is the most common and effective use.

**Cover seasonal inventory.** Stock up before your busy season and repay as sales come in. This is especially effective for retail, e-commerce, and food service businesses.

**Handle unexpected costs.** Equipment breakdowns, emergency repairs, or sudden opportunities that require fast capital.

**Take supplier discounts.** Many suppliers offer 2% to 5% discounts for early payment. If your line of credit costs less than the discount saves, it is a net positive trade.

**Do not use for long-term fixed assets.** If you are buying equipment you will use for 5+ years, [equipment financing](/business-loans/equipment-financing) typically offers better rates and terms. A line of credit should not fund long-term investments.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Maxing out your credit line.** Using 100% of your available credit signals financial stress to lenders and may trigger rate increases or limit reductions. Aim to stay below 70% utilization.

**Using it for the wrong purpose.** A line of credit is for short-term, variable needs. Using it for a long-term real estate purchase or major equipment buy means you are paying more than you need to.

**Ignoring all-in costs.** That 12% interest rate looks good until you add the 2% draw fee, $150 annual fee, and required minimum draws. Calculate the total cost, not just the rate.

**Not understanding variable rates.** Many lines of credit have variable rates tied to the prime rate. If rates rise, your cost rises. Ask whether you can lock portions of your draws at a fixed rate.

**Letting the line go dormant.** Some lenders charge inactivity fees or may close the line entirely. If you have a line of credit, use it periodically (even small amounts) to keep it active.

## The Bottom Line

A business line of credit is the most versatile financing product available to small businesses. It gives you access to capital when you need it, costs nothing when you do not, and replenishes as you repay. The key is qualifying at the right time, choosing the right lender, and using it strategically for short-term needs that generate returns for your business.
