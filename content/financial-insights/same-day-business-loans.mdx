Sometimes you need money now. A piece of equipment breaks, a supplier offers a time-limited bulk discount, or payroll is due Friday and a client's payment did not clear. In these moments, the best financing option is the one that gets funds into your account fast enough to solve the problem.

This guide covers every fast-funding option available to small businesses, what each one actually costs, and how to tell when speed is worth paying a premium versus when you should slow down and shop for better terms.

## How Fast Can You Actually Get Business Funding?

Here is the realistic timeline for each major product type:

| Product | Typical Funding Speed | Fastest Possible |
|---|---|---|
| MCA (Merchant Cash Advance) | 1 to 3 business days | Same day |
| Line of Credit (existing) | Same day draw | Immediate |
| Revenue-Based Loans | 1 to 3 business days | Same day |
| Online Term Loans | 1 to 5 business days | Next day |
| Invoice Factoring | 1 to 5 business days | 24 hours |
| Equipment Financing | 3 to 10 business days | 3 days |
| Bank Term Loans | 1 to 4 weeks | 1 week |
| SBA Loans | 4 to 13 weeks | 4 weeks |

Important: "Same day" usually means same business day if your application is submitted and approved before noon. Weekends and holidays do not count. Funding also depends on your bank's processing times.

## Fastest Business Funding Options

### 1. Existing Business Line of Credit (Fastest)

If you already have a [business line of credit](/business-loans/lines-of-credit), drawing on it is the fastest way to access capital. Most lines allow same-day or next-day draws through an online portal, and some offer immediate ACH transfers.

**Speed:** Immediate to same day
**Cost:** Your existing line rate (7% to 25% APR)
**Takeaway:** This is why establishing a line of credit before you need it is one of the smartest moves a business owner can make.

### 2. Revenue-Based Loans

Online lenders that use bank statement underwriting can approve and fund based on your revenue alone. The application is often a single page plus bank account connection.

**Speed:** 1 to 3 business days, sometimes same day
**Cost:** 15% to 40% effective APR
**What you need:** 6+ months in business, $10,000+/month in revenue, connected bank account
**Best for:** Established businesses with consistent deposits that need capital fast

### 3. Merchant Cash Advances

MCAs are the fastest lump-sum option for businesses without an existing credit line. Approval can happen in hours, not days.

**Speed:** Same day to 3 business days
**Cost:** 40% to 150%+ effective APR (1.1 to 1.5 factor rate)
**What you need:** $5,000+/month in revenue or credit card sales, 3+ months in business
**Best for:** Genuine emergencies when no other option is available in time

The speed of an MCA comes at a steep price. On a $50,000 advance with a 1.35 factor rate, you repay $67,500 over roughly 6 to 12 months. Use our [MCA payback calculator](/tools/mca-payback-calculator) to see the real numbers before you sign.

### 4. Online Term Loans

Fintech lenders like those in the <a href="https://www.responsiblelending.org/" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">responsible lending space</a> have streamlined the term loan process significantly. Many can approve and fund within 1 to 3 business days.

**Speed:** 1 to 5 business days
**Cost:** 8% to 30% APR
**What you need:** 1+ year in business, $100,000+ annual revenue, 600+ credit score
**Best for:** Borrowers who can wait 48 hours and want significantly better rates than MCAs

### 5. Invoice Factoring

If you have outstanding B2B invoices, [factoring](/financial-insights/invoice-factoring-loans) can convert them to cash within 1 to 3 business days. The first factoring transaction takes longest due to setup. Subsequent advances are typically next-day.

**Speed:** 1 to 5 business days (first time), 24 hours (ongoing)
**Cost:** 1% to 5% per month factoring fee
**What you need:** Outstanding invoices from creditworthy business customers
**Best for:** B2B companies in construction, manufacturing, staffing, or professional services

## The Real Cost of Speed

Speed costs money. Here is what a $50,000 loan looks like across different timelines:

| Speed | Product | Typical Total Repayment | Extra Cost vs. Cheapest |
|---|---|---|---|
| Same day | MCA (1.35 factor) | $67,500 | $12,700 more |
| 2-3 days | Revenue-based loan | $60,000 to $65,000 | $5,200 to $10,200 more |
| 1 week | Online term loan (2-year) | $57,500 to $62,000 | $2,700 to $7,200 more |
| 4-6 weeks | Bank term loan (3-year) | $54,800 | Baseline |

That $12,700 premium for same-day MCA funding is real money. It is the cost of not planning ahead. For some emergencies, it is worth it. For most situations, waiting even 48 hours saves thousands.

## When Fast Funding Makes Sense

**Emergency equipment replacement.** Your delivery truck breaks down and you need a replacement this week to keep fulfilling orders. Lost revenue from downtime exceeds the premium for fast funding.

**Time-sensitive supplier deals.** A supplier offers 30% off a bulk order but the deal closes in 3 days. The discount more than covers the cost of a short-term draw.

**Payroll emergencies.** A major client's payment is delayed and payroll is due. Missing payroll destroys employee trust and can trigger legal issues. Speed is justified.

**Revenue-generating opportunities.** A large contract lands but requires upfront investment in materials or labor. The contract profit far exceeds the cost of fast financing.

## When to Slow Down Instead

**You have more than a week.** If your need is not truly urgent, take the time to compare offers. Even 5 business days opens up significantly better options. Use our [loan comparison tool](/tools/business-loan-comparison-tool) to evaluate side by side.

**The need is not clearly defined.** If you are not sure exactly what the money is for, fast funding becomes expensive guesswork. Slow down, clarify the need, and match it to the right product.

**You are in a cycle of fast borrowing.** If you have taken out an MCA or fast loan in the last 6 months and are seeking another, the underlying issue is likely cash flow management, not a funding gap. Our [cash flow forecast tool](/tools/cash-flow-forecast-tool) can help you identify the root problem.

**The "urgency" is manufactured.** If a lender tells you the offer expires today, that is a sales tactic. No legitimate offer disappears overnight. Read our guide on [spotting predatory lenders](/financial-insights/how-to-spot-predatory-business-loan).

## How to Be Ready for Fast Funding Before You Need It

The best time to prepare for a financial emergency is before one happens.

**Establish a line of credit now.** Even if you do not need it today, having an approved line of credit means same-day access when an emergency hits, at a fraction of the cost of an MCA.

**Keep financials current.** Updated bank statements, P&L, and tax returns ready to go means faster processing when you apply. Our [loan document checklist](/tools/loan-document-checklist) covers exactly what to keep on file.

**Build relationships with lenders.** Having an existing relationship with a bank or online lender means faster underwriting when you need it. The <a href="https://www.score.org/resource/article/find-mentor" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">SCORE mentorship program</a> can connect you with advisors who help identify the right lenders for your business.

**Maintain a cash reserve.** Three to six months of operating expenses in savings eliminates most emergencies entirely. Use our [cash flow forecast tool](/tools/cash-flow-forecast-tool) to calculate what that number looks like for your business.

## The Bottom Line

Same-day business funding exists, but it comes at a price. The smart strategy is to prepare in advance so you rarely need it: establish a line of credit, keep documents ready, and build cash reserves.
