Purchasing equipment is one of the largest capital expenses most small businesses face. Whether you need commercial vehicles, manufacturing machinery, medical devices, restaurant appliances, or technology infrastructure, paying the full cost upfront can strain your cash reserves. [Equipment financing](/business-loans/equipment-financing) lets you spread that cost over time while putting the asset to work immediately.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about financing business equipment, including loan options, qualification requirements, tax benefits, and strategies for getting the best terms.

## How Equipment Financing Works

With an equipment loan, a lender provides funds specifically to purchase business equipment. The equipment itself typically serves as collateral for the loan, which reduces risk for the lender and often results in more favorable terms compared to unsecured borrowing.

You make fixed monthly payments over a set term, usually between 2 and 7 years. Once the loan is fully repaid, you own the equipment outright. Because the equipment secures the loan, equipment financing often has:

- Higher approval rates than unsecured loans
- Lower interest rates
- Faster approval processes
- More flexible credit requirements

### The Equipment Financing Process

1. **Identify equipment needs and get vendor quotes**
2. **Gather financial documentation**
3. **Submit application to lenders**
4. **Receive approval (often same-day)**
5. **Lender pays vendor directly or reimburses you**
6. **Make fixed monthly payments**
7. **Own equipment free and clear at loan end**

## Equipment Loans vs. Equipment Leases

These two options serve different needs, and the best choice depends on your business situation.

### Equipment Loans

With a loan, you own the equipment from day one and build equity with each payment.

**Advantages:**
- Full ownership and equity building
- Can modify equipment as needed
- No mileage or usage restrictions
- Asset appears on your balance sheet
- Can sell equipment anytime
- May qualify for Section 179 tax deduction

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher monthly payments than leases
- Responsible for maintenance costs
- Risk of owning outdated equipment
- Potentially harder to qualify

**Best for:**
- Equipment with long useful life (7+ years)
- Assets that retain value well
- Equipment you'll customize or modify
- Businesses wanting to build assets

### Equipment Leases

With a lease, you use the equipment without owning it. At the end of the term, you typically have options: return it, buy it at fair market value, or continue leasing.

**Types of leases:**

**Capital Lease (Finance Lease):**
- Functions similar to a loan
- Ownership transfers at end of term
- Full tax deductions available
- Equipment on your balance sheet

**Operating Lease:**
- True rental arrangement
- Return equipment at term end
- Lower monthly payments
- Equipment off your balance sheet

**Advantages:**
- Lower monthly payments
- Easier qualification
- Upgrade to newer models at term end
- Maintenance may be included
- Operating leases keep debt off balance sheet

**Disadvantages:**
- No equity building
- Higher total cost over time
- Mileage/usage restrictions
- May have early termination fees

**Best for:**
- Technology that becomes obsolete quickly
- Equipment you'll use for a specific project
- Testing equipment before committing to purchase
- Businesses that want to preserve capital

### Lease vs. Buy Decision Framework

Consider leasing when:
- Equipment will be obsolete in 3-5 years
- You need the latest technology
- Cash flow is tight but revenue is growing
- You prefer predictable budgeting without maintenance surprises

Consider buying when:
- Equipment has 7+ year useful life
- You want to build business equity
- Tax deductions are valuable to you
- You'll customize or heavily modify the equipment

## What Lenders Evaluate

When reviewing an equipment financing application, lenders typically consider:

### Credit Score

Both personal and business credit matter for small businesses. Expect these general thresholds:

| Credit Score | Typical Outcome |
|--------------|-----------------|
| 700+ | Best rates, easy approval |
| 650-699 | Good rates, standard approval |
| 600-649 | Higher rates, may need larger down payment |
| 550-599 | Limited options, higher rates |
| Below 550 | Difficult, may need co-signer or larger down payment |

If your credit needs work, see our guide on [improving your credit score quickly](/financial-insights/how-to-improve-credit-fast).

### Time in Business

Most lenders prefer at least one year of operating history, though some work with newer businesses:

- **2+ years:** Full access to most lenders
- **1-2 years:** Most equipment finance companies
- **6-12 months:** Some alternative lenders
- **Under 6 months:** Limited options, startup-focused lenders only

### Revenue and Cash Flow

Lenders want to see that your monthly income comfortably covers the proposed payment along with your existing obligations. They typically look for:

- Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) of 1.25x or higher
- Consistent monthly deposits
- Stable or growing revenue trend
- Minimal overdrafts or bounced payments

### Equipment Type and Value

The asset being financed affects approval odds because it's the collateral. Factors include:

- **Resale value:** Equipment with active secondary markets poses less risk
- **Depreciation rate:** Slower depreciation is better for lenders
- **Industry demand:** Popular equipment types are easier to finance
- **New vs. used:** New equipment often qualifies for better terms
- **Useful life:** Loan terms typically can't exceed the equipment's expected useful life

## Benefits of Equipment Financing

### Preserve Working Capital

Rather than depleting your cash reserves, you keep funds available for payroll, inventory, marketing, and other operational needs. This is especially valuable for growing businesses that need cash for multiple purposes.

### Tax Advantages

**Section 179 Deduction:**
The IRS allows businesses to deduct the full purchase price of qualifying equipment in the year it's placed in service, rather than depreciating it over time. For 2024:
- Maximum deduction: $1,160,000
- Phase-out threshold: $2,890,000 in total equipment purchases
- Must be new or "new to you" equipment
- Must be used for business more than 50% of the time

**Bonus Depreciation:**
In addition to Section 179, businesses can take bonus depreciation on qualifying assets. This allows additional first-year deductions on new equipment.

**Interest Deduction:**
Interest paid on equipment loans is generally tax-deductible as a business expense.

Consult with a tax professional to understand how these benefits apply to your specific situation.

### Predictable Budgeting

Fixed monthly payments make it straightforward to plan your expenses and manage cash flow. Unlike variable expenses, you know exactly what your equipment costs each month.

### Access to Better Equipment

Financing enables you to acquire higher-quality assets than you might afford with a cash purchase. Better equipment often means:
- Improved productivity and efficiency
- Lower maintenance costs
- Competitive advantage
- Better employee satisfaction

### Hedge Against Inflation

Financing locks in today's price for equipment that may cost more in the future. Your fixed payments stay the same even as equipment prices rise.

## Steps to Secure Equipment Financing

### Step 1: Identify Exactly What You Need

Before approaching lenders:
- Get detailed quotes from equipment vendors
- Know the total purchase price including delivery, installation, and setup
- Understand warranty coverage and maintenance requirements
- Consider whether new or used equipment makes sense

### Step 2: Review Your Financial Position

Prepare for the application process:
- Pull your personal and business credit reports
- Gather recent bank statements (3-6 months)
- Compile tax returns (2-3 years if available)
- Prepare profit and loss statements
- Calculate your current debt obligations

### Step 3: Compare Lender Options

Different lenders offer different terms:

**Banks and Credit Unions:**
- Best rates for well-qualified borrowers
- Longer approval process (2-4 weeks)
- May require existing relationship
- Often more documentation requirements

**Equipment Finance Companies:**
- Specialize in equipment lending
- Understand equipment values and industries
- Competitive rates
- Often faster than banks

**Online Lenders:**
- Fastest approval (often same-day)
- More flexible requirements
- May have higher rates
- Technology-driven process

**Equipment Dealers/Manufacturers:**
- Convenient financing at point of sale
- May offer promotional rates
- Limited to that vendor's equipment
- Compare to third-party financing

### Step 4: Understand Total Cost

Look beyond the monthly payment to calculate the true cost:

- **Interest rate (APR):** The annual cost of borrowing
- **Origination fees:** Typically 1-3% of loan amount
- **Documentation fees:** Administrative charges
- **Early payoff penalties:** Fees for paying ahead of schedule
- **End-of-term costs:** For leases, potential purchase prices or return fees

Calculate total amount paid over the loan term versus the equipment's cash price.

### Step 5: Submit Your Application

Provide required documentation:
- Business and personal tax returns
- Bank statements
- Financial statements
- Equipment quote or invoice
- Business licenses and registration
- Proof of insurance

Be prepared to explain how the equipment will be used and how it will benefit your business.

### Step 6: Review and Accept Terms

Before signing:
- Confirm monthly payment and total cost
- Check for prepayment penalties
- Understand personal guarantee requirements
- Verify equipment delivery and funding timeline
- Review insurance requirements

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Overfinancing

Borrowing more than the equipment is worth can leave you "upside down" if the asset depreciates faster than you pay down the loan. This is especially problematic for technology and equipment with short useful lives.

### Ignoring Total Cost of Ownership

The loan payment is only part of the expense. Factor in:
- Insurance requirements
- Maintenance and repairs
- Operating costs (fuel, supplies, electricity)
- Training for employees
- Eventual replacement costs

### Skipping the Fine Print

Watch for problematic terms:
- **Balloon payments:** Large payment due at end of term
- **Variable rates:** Payments that can increase over time
- **Personal guarantees:** Your personal assets at risk
- **UCC filings:** Liens that appear on business credit
- **Usage restrictions:** Limits on how you use the equipment

### Not Shopping Around

Rates and terms vary significantly. A difference of even 2-3% in interest rate can save thousands over the loan term. Get at least 3 quotes before committing.

### Financing the Wrong Equipment

Consider whether financing makes sense for your specific situation:
- Will this equipment be obsolete before the loan is paid off?
- Is the equipment essential or just nice-to-have?
- Could you rent or lease short-term instead?
- Are there used alternatives that would work?

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can startups qualify for equipment financing?

Yes, though options may be more limited. Strategies for startups:
- Work with lenders specializing in startup financing
- Consider equipment dealer programs (often more flexible)
- Provide a larger down payment (20-30%)
- Expect to pay higher interest rates
- Explore SBA microloan programs for equipment

See our guide on [first-time business loans](/financial-insights/first-time-business-loans) for more startup financing strategies.

### How much can I borrow?

Most equipment loans cover up to 100% of the equipment cost. Some considerations:
- Lenders may cap at 80-90% for used equipment
- Down payments of 10-20% may yield better rates
- Soft costs (delivery, installation) sometimes included
- Maximum loan amounts vary by lender ($50K to $10M+)

### How quickly can I get funded?

Approval and funding timelines vary:
- **Online lenders:** Same-day to 3 days
- **Equipment finance companies:** 3-7 days
- **Banks and credit unions:** 2-4 weeks
- **SBA-backed loans:** 4-8 weeks

For urgent needs, online lenders and equipment finance companies offer the fastest path.

### What credit score do I need?

While minimums vary by lender:
- **650+:** Access to most mainstream options
- **600-649:** Limited options, higher rates
- **Below 600:** Consider co-signers, larger down payments, or credit building first

Equipment financing is often more accessible than unsecured financing because the equipment provides collateral.

### Should I finance new or used equipment?

Both have advantages:

**New Equipment:**
- Latest technology and features
- Full manufacturer warranty
- Potentially better financing rates
- Section 179 eligible
- No unknown maintenance history

**Used Equipment:**
- Lower purchase price
- Slower depreciation (already depreciated)
- May still have warranty remaining
- Can find high-quality, lightly-used options
- Faster availability (no manufacturing wait)

Consider the equipment's depreciation curve. Some assets lose value quickly in year one, making used equipment a better value.

### Can I refinance equipment I already own?

Yes, through equipment refinancing:
- Use existing equipment as collateral for a new loan
- Access cash while continuing to use the equipment
- Potentially get better terms if your credit has improved
- May consolidate multiple equipment loans

This can be a good option for [accessing working capital](/business-loans/asset-backed-loans) without taking on unsecured debt.

## Alternative Financing Options

If equipment financing doesn't fit your situation, consider:

**[Business term loans](/business-loans/term-loans):** General-purpose financing that can be used for equipment along with other needs.

**[Lines of credit](/business-loans/lines-of-credit):** Flexible funds you can draw on for equipment and other expenses.

**Equipment rental:** Short-term access without long-term commitment—good for seasonal or project-specific needs.
